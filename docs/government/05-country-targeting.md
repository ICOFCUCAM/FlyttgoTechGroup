# Country-Targeting Prioritisation Framework

Methodology for ranking jurisdictions for FlyttGo's public-sector
outreach. The framework drives where the public-sector engagement
desk spends time, which capability briefs are translated first, and
which countries get pre-emptive sovereign-deployment readiness
investment.

The framework is applied quarterly by the public-sector engagement
desk and reviewed annually by the leadership group. Each
jurisdiction has a documented score, a tier assignment, and a named
engagement lead.

---

## Scoring dimensions

Each jurisdiction is scored on six dimensions, each on a 1–5 scale
(1 = unfavourable / 5 = favourable). Weights are applied per
dimension to derive a composite score. The weights reflect what
actually predicts a workable engagement, not what reads well in a
strategy deck.

### D1 · Programme demand signal — weight 0.25

What public materials, RFPs and ministerial communications indicate
about the jurisdiction's appetite for the kind of platform FlyttGo
operates.

| Score | Signal |
|---|---|
| 5 | Active RFP or programme charter explicitly aligned to one or more FlyttGo modules |
| 4 | Published digital-strategy document names an aligned capability without a current procurement |
| 3 | Sectoral consultation issued; programme not yet defined |
| 2 | General digitalisation rhetoric; no specific programme |
| 1 | No public material on platform-class infrastructure |

### D2 · Procurement accessibility — weight 0.20

How approachable the jurisdiction's public procurement is to a
non-domestic vendor.

| Score | Signal |
|---|---|
| 5 | Open EU/EEA procurement; non-domestic vendors regularly win |
| 4 | National procurement with documented framework agreements open to EU vendors |
| 3 | National procurement with disclosed framework but historical bias toward domestic vendors |
| 2 | Closed procurement with case-by-case access |
| 1 | Procurement reserved for domestic or affiliated vendors |

### D3 · Regulatory clarity — weight 0.15

How well-defined the jurisdiction's public-sector data, identity and
payment regulation is.

| Score | Signal |
|---|---|
| 5 | EU member state — eIDAS, GDPR, PSD2 all apply by default |
| 4 | EEA / Council of Europe alignment; equivalent frameworks |
| 3 | Sectoral regulator + national data-protection authority both operational |
| 2 | Regulators exist but enforcement is uneven |
| 1 | Regulatory framework still in legislative draft |

### D4 · Sovereignty posture — weight 0.15

How much weight the jurisdiction places on data residency, key
custody and Cloud-Act exposure. Higher score = stronger fit for
FlyttGo's sovereign deployment mode.

| Score | Signal |
|---|---|
| 5 | Public mandate for in-jurisdiction hosting; sovereign-cloud programme funded |
| 4 | Strong residency policy; sovereign hosting offered as procurement option |
| 3 | Residency-aware but no specific sovereign programme |
| 2 | Residency mentioned in policy without enforcement |
| 1 | Hyperscaler-default; no sovereign concern raised |

### D5 · Existing FlyttGo footprint — weight 0.15

What FlyttGo can already point to in the jurisdiction or a
neighbouring one with reciprocal recognition.

| Score | Signal |
|---|---|
| 5 | One or more production deployments in jurisdiction |
| 4 | Production deployment in same regulatory zone (e.g. another EU member state) |
| 3 | Reference programme in adjacent jurisdiction |
| 2 | Pre-engagement contacts in jurisdiction |
| 1 | No prior engagement |

### D6 · Operating-cost & language overhead — weight 0.10

The cost of effective engagement: language, currency, time-zone,
business norms.

| Score | Signal |
|---|---|
| 5 | English working language in public sector; EUR / GBP / USD |
| 4 | Strong English working capability; local currency manageable |
| 3 | Local language required for formal documents; translation overhead |
| 2 | Local language required throughout; translation team needed |
| 1 | Language and time-zone overhead exceeds engagement cost ceiling |

## Composite score & tier assignment

Composite = `0.25·D1 + 0.20·D2 + 0.15·D3 + 0.15·D4 + 0.15·D5 + 0.10·D6`

Score ranges and corresponding tiers:

| Composite range | Tier | Engagement posture |
|---|---|---|
| 4.30 — 5.00 | **T1 — Active** | Named engagement lead; quarterly outreach cadence; capability brief translated into local language; pilot-readiness investment |
| 3.50 — 4.29 | **T2 — Cultivate** | Named engagement lead; semi-annual outreach; capability brief in EN; warm-introduction prioritised |
| 2.70 — 3.49 | **T3 — Watch** | Tracked by the desk; quarterly score refresh; no proactive outreach; respond to inbound |
| 1.90 — 2.69 | **T4 — Defer** | Recorded but not tracked; no resource allocated; revisit at annual review |
| < 1.90      | **T5 — Out** | Excluded; revisit only on material policy change |

A jurisdiction's tier is reviewed quarterly. Movement between tiers
requires a written rationale entered into the prioritisation
register.

## Indicative tier placement (illustrative)

The actual prioritisation register lives in the engagement desk's
operations file and is updated quarterly. The placements below are
illustrative — they show the shape of the framework at work, not
binding assignments.

### T1 — Active

- Norway · NO — home jurisdiction, multi-module footprint, sovereign
  programme funded, regulatory clarity (EEA + national)
- Sweden · SE — Nordic neighbour, EU-aligned, established footprint
- Denmark · DK — Nordic neighbour, public-sector digital maturity high
- Finland · FI — strong sovereign cloud programme, EU-aligned
- United Kingdom · GB — large public sector, post-Brexit
  framework-agreement structure favours non-EU vendors
- Germany · DE — large addressable market, GDPR & BSI-aligned
  sovereign cloud programme

### T2 — Cultivate

- France · FR — strong sovereign-cloud framework (SecNumCloud)
- Netherlands · NL — large public sector, EU-aligned
- Estonia · EE — pioneer e-government jurisdiction
- Ireland · IE — financial-operations cluster, EU-aligned
- United Arab Emirates · AE — established sovereign-deployment
  market, FlyttGo regional presence
- Saudi Arabia · SA — funded sovereign-cloud programme (NCA)

### T3 — Watch

- Belgium · BE — mid-scale, programme cadence slow
- Austria · AT — mid-scale, EU-aligned
- Kenya · KE — strong digital programme, growing public-sector
  modernisation
- Nigeria · NG — large addressable market, growing regulatory
  clarity
- South Africa · ZA — sovereign-cloud emerging, FlyttGo presence
- Egypt · EG — programme demand signal increasing

### T4 — Defer

- Smaller EU member states with limited public-sector budget
- Jurisdictions without an established sovereign-cloud framework
- Markets where the language and translation overhead exceeds the
  engagement-cost ceiling

### T5 — Out

- Jurisdictions under active sanctions
- Jurisdictions where Cloud-Act exposure is incompatible with
  FlyttGo's sovereign-deployment posture
- Jurisdictions with regulatory frameworks that conflict with
  GDPR / eIDAS / SOC 2 II to the point of unworkability

## Engagement allocations per tier

The desk runs to a fixed quarterly capacity; the framework allocates
that capacity across tiers. Indicative allocations:

| Tier | Engagement-desk hours / quarter | Capability-brief language coverage | Outreach cadence |
|---|---|---|---|
| T1 — Active | 40+ hrs per jurisdiction | Local language + EN | Quarterly + RFP-triggered |
| T2 — Cultivate | 12–20 hrs per jurisdiction | EN; local on request | Semi-annual + warm intros |
| T3 — Watch | 4–8 hrs per jurisdiction | EN | Inbound only |
| T4 — Defer | 0 hrs (tracked, not engaged) | n/a | None |
| T5 — Out | 0 hrs (excluded) | n/a | None |

A jurisdiction's allocation is set at the quarterly review and
locked for that quarter. Mid-quarter changes require an exceptional
note in the prioritisation register.

## Trigger events that move a jurisdiction up a tier

The framework reacts to specific public events. Each of the
following automatically promotes the jurisdiction one tier in the
following quarterly review:

- A formal RFP issued by a ministry whose programme aligns to one
  or more FlyttGo modules
- A public consultation opened on platform-class infrastructure in
  the recipient's policy domain
- A new sovereign-cloud framework published by the jurisdiction's
  digital-affairs ministry
- A first warm introduction from a regional partner or member of
  the FlyttGo board
- A first production deployment landing in the jurisdiction (moves
  D5 to score 5)

Trigger events that move a jurisdiction **down** a tier:

- Sanctions or material trade restrictions against the jurisdiction
- Regulatory framework reversal (e.g. data-protection authority
  weakening)
- Two consecutive quarters of no movement in D1 (programme demand
  signal)
- A FlyttGo decision to deprioritise the regulatory zone

## Governance

- The prioritisation register is owned by the public-sector
  engagement desk lead.
- Quarterly review happens within 14 days of each calendar quarter
  close.
- Annual review happens within 30 days of fiscal year close, with
  the leadership group.
- Movements between tiers, allocations, and the rationale are all
  recorded in the register; the register is itself an audit
  artefact and is retained for 7 years.
- The framework itself is reviewed every two years, or earlier if a
  material methodological flaw surfaces.

---

## What the framework is not

This framework is a prioritisation tool. It is not a market-sizing
exercise; it is not a sales-pipeline tool; it is not a TAM
calculation. It tells the engagement desk where to spend its hours
this quarter. Any use beyond that scope is out of scope for the
framework and must be done with separate methodology.
